What We Learned from the Pitch Experience
2016 02 29




Being able to present a precise, impressive pitch is very important for startups. In fact, all the knowledge about doing a pitch can be applied to every aspect in the business.

Last week, we had an opportunity to introduce ourself with Christopher Ireland, Elizabeth Glenewinkel (gravitytank), Katy Mogal (Fitbit), David Hendee (Carbon 5) , Steve Diller (Scansion) and Michael Lemme (Duncan/Channon).
It was a great experience. We received many stimulating feedback and would love to share with you, fashion friends:

Most people were impressive about our choices on the font and the layout. The title font was "handmade" by the Designer, Ming Jaturuntawanichya. The brushy, bold attempt on the font attracted the most audience's eyes. With the bright, vivid color, the pitch deck perfectly reflected in the personality of our startup– compelling and immersive.

We also received some feedback to improve our pitch deck.

Providing Cases Study, Such as Videos:
Apart from the value, culture, and how our agency works, we should include a clear and intriguing example about our product to convince the potential clients.

Revise the Wording
Another lesson we learned from the presentation is that we need to make sure the language uses are 100% correct. A pitch with noticeable grammatical errors make it unprofessional.

To Be Precise on the service design process:
Instead of introducing the process with general terms like consulting, researching and the like, we should make it more detailed. Maybe talking about the process with a real project is a better strategy.

Selected a new Typeface:
We tried to make the hierarchy of the content clearly. However, we highlighted the title of each slice too much. They caught people’s attention, so that people may ignore the content below.

The ability of communication is very crucial for a designer. We are very appreciate every feedback to make Sapphire Agency better.
